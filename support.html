Support Our Mission

Help professionals and communities detect disease earlier, make better care decisions, and expand access to reliable diagnostics and education.

Why Support the Foundation

The Aesculapius Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit advancing One Health education, veterinary diagnostic literacy, infectious disease preparedness, independent evaluation, and access-focused support for underserved or cost-sensitive care settings.

Support helps fund practical educational resources, independent evaluation and product evaluation studies, disease preparedness initiatives, and access programs for veterinary practices, farms, shelters, students, caregivers, animal owners, and communities.

Donors and partners can help close a real gap: professionals and care communities need trustworthy, independent information about diagnostics, surveillance, workflow, cost, and clinical value before decisions are made.

Diagnostic Literacy & Education

Support may help fund learning resources, webinars, toolkits, micro-credentials, and decision-support guides that make diagnostic knowledge easier to understand and apply.

Disease Surveillance & Preparedness

Support may help develop One Health education, zoonotic disease awareness resources, early detection training, infectious disease response simulations, and community-based preparedness programs.

Independent Evaluation

Support may help make non-commercial diagnostic evaluation data available to veterinary practices, educators, shelters, farms, and underserved care settings without charge.

Access, Affordability & Community Impact

Support may help expand diagnostic education, equipment access, implementation guidance, and cost-sensitive care initiatives for shelters, high-volume low-cost clinics, rural practices, mobile services, farms, and animal owners.

Ways to Support

The Foundation expects most major support to come through check, ACH, wire transfer, corporate or manufacturer support, restricted program grants, and in-kind contributions rather than small online donations.

For ACH, wire, check instructions, restricted program gifts, corporate sponsorship, manufacturer-supported evaluation studies, equipment donations, software credits, educational sponsorships, or in-kind support, please contact us.

Independence and Stewardship

Foundation support does not provide product endorsement, editorial control, preferential findings, or sponsor control over validation methodology, educational content, grant decisions, or publication decisions.

The Foundation maintains educational, editorial, and methodological independence and will seek appropriate transparency for donor, sponsor, manufacturer, distributor, or partner support.
