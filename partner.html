Partner With Us

Collaborate with the Foundation to advance independent evaluation, One Health education, and access to reliable care.

Why Partner With the Foundation

The Aesculapius Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit advancing One Health education, veterinary diagnostic literacy, infectious disease preparedness, and access to reliable care. Our programs are grounded in independence, transparency, and practical value for veterinary practices, farms, shelters, students, caregivers, and communities.

We welcome mission-aligned collaboration with manufacturers, distributors, veterinary practices, educational institutions, professional associations, community organizations, farms, shelters, and funding partners. Effective partnerships help the Foundation expand its reach while preserving the independence that makes its work credible and valuable to the professionals and communities it serves.

Partnership Opportunities

Ways to Partner

Evaluation Program

Manufacturers and distributors may support or participate in the Foundation's independent diagnostic evaluation and product evaluation program. Partner-supported studies follow the same non-commercial protocols as Foundation-initiated work. Partners receive no influence over study design, findings, or dissemination — and gain credible, practitioner-centered third-party performance data.

Equipment & Supply Donations

Organizations may donate diagnostic instruments, veterinary supplies, consumables, or other equipment to support the Foundation's Access, Affordability & Equipment Grant Program. Donated resources are distributed to qualified shelters, rural practices, high-volume low-cost clinics, farms, and other underserved care settings.

Educational Partnerships

Accredited education providers, professional associations, veterinary schools, and community health organizations may collaborate on diagnostic literacy resources, infectious disease preparedness education, and One Health programming. All educational content maintains Foundation editorial and methodological independence.

Funding & Grant Support

Corporations, foundations, and individual donors may support specific program areas — including independent evaluation studies, access grants, educational resource development, and disease preparedness initiatives — through charitable contributions or designated grants.

What Partners Can Expect

  • Clear documentation of partnership terms, scope, and independence requirements
  • No editorial or methodological influence over Foundation findings or educational content
  • Transparent public acknowledgment of partner support, consistent with Foundation policy
  • Direct access to Foundation staff for program coordination and scheduling
  • Opportunities to reach veterinary practices, farms, shelters, educators, and care communities through Foundation programs
  • Alignment with a credentialed 501(c)(3) nonprofit with a Candid Gold transparency seal

Independence Is the Foundation's Value

The Foundation's usefulness to practitioners, educators, and communities depends on its independence from commercial interests. All partnership structures — including evaluation study participation, equipment donations, educational collaborations, and funding support — are designed to preserve that independence.

Partners whose products, programs, or resources perform well under honest, transparent evaluation gain something no marketing effort can replicate: credible, practitioner-centered evidence that practitioners, educators, and communities can trust.

Start a Partnership Conversation

The Foundation welcomes preliminary inquiries from mission-aligned organizations. Whether you are interested in participating in a validation study, donating equipment, supporting a program area, or exploring a broader educational collaboration, we would like to hear from you.
