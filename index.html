The Aesculapius Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing healthcare education, whole-person health literacy, professional development, and community empowerment.

Healthcare education, whole-person health literacy, professional development, and community empowerment.

The Foundation’s mission is to provide accessible, high-quality educational resources and initiatives that support excellence in medical practice, promote evidence-informed approaches to health and wellness, improve health literacy, and empower healthcare professionals, students, caregivers, and communities with knowledge that supports better health outcomes.

Named in honor of Asclepius, the ancient symbol of healing, the Foundation is committed to building a healthier world through lifelong learning, compassionate education, scientific integrity, and informed community engagement.

Our Focus

Education that supports better understanding and better care.

Healthcare Education

Accessible educational resources for healthcare professionals, students, caregivers, and communities.

Whole-Person Health Literacy

Evidence-informed education about the physical, emotional, mental, social, environmental, and lifestyle factors that influence health.

Professional Development

Lifelong learning, ethical practice, clinical excellence, leadership, and innovation across the health sciences.

Community Empowerment

Initiatives that improve health literacy, expand access to reliable information, and help communities make better-informed decisions.

Built on experience

A renewed mission with a history in healthcare education.

The Foundation has prior experience developing independent medical education and CME-related programming in collaboration with accredited providers, faculty experts, and healthcare-sector partners.

Prior work included

  • Needs assessment and educational planning Translating learning gaps into educational objectives and program designs.
  • Live and online programs Supporting lectures, workshops, webinar-based education, and enduring educational materials.
  • Program execution and evaluation Coordinating faculty, audiences, logistics, communications, and post-program evaluation.

Why It Matters

Better health outcomes begin with better knowledge. The Aesculapius Foundation exists to make healthcare education more accessible, practical, and useful — not only for professionals and students, but also for communities seeking clearer, more reliable health information.

By supporting healthcare education, whole-person health literacy, professional development, and community empowerment, the Foundation aims to encourage a more informed, compassionate, prevention-oriented approach to health.
