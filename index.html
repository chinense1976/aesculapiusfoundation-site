The Aesculapius Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing healthcare education, veterinary diagnostic literacy, professional development, and community empowerment across the full spectrum of health — human, animal, and environmental.

Named for Asclepius, the ancient symbol of healing, the Foundation supports lifelong learning, evidence-informed practice, and expanded access to the knowledge and tools that improve health outcomes.

Our Focus

Education that supports better understanding and better care.

Healthcare & Veterinary Education

Accessible educational resources for healthcare and veterinary professionals, students, technicians, caregivers, and communities.

Veterinary Diagnostic Literacy

Practical education on point-of-care diagnostic instruments — selection, performance, workflow integration, and clinical interpretation for veterinary practices of all sizes.

Professional Development

Continuing education, hands-on training, and practical skill-building for veterinary and healthcare professionals at all career stages.

Access & Equity Programs

Initiatives that expand access to diagnostic tools and reliable health information in underserved settings — including animal shelters, rural practices, and high-volume low-cost clinics.

Built on experience

A renewed mission with a history in healthcare education.

The Foundation has prior experience developing independent medical education and CME-related programming in collaboration with accredited providers, faculty experts, and healthcare-sector partners. We are now expanding that work into veterinary health and diagnostic education — areas where accessible, independent information is in short supply.

Why It Matters

Better health outcomes begin with better knowledge. The Aesculapius Foundation exists to make healthcare and veterinary education more accessible, practical, and useful — not only for professionals and students, but also for communities and practices seeking clearer, more reliable information about the diagnostic tools they use every day.

Support accessible healthcare and veterinary education.

The Foundation is building practical resources, validation studies, and professional development initiatives that help people and practices make better-informed decisions.
