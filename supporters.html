Supporters & Technology Resources

Recognition of nonprofit technology programs, in-kind resources, and prior charitable support that help reduce costs and expand the Foundation's educational, scientific, and access-oriented work.

Recognition for transparency and appreciation

The Aesculapius Foundation is grateful for nonprofit technology programs, in-kind resources, and prior charitable support that help reduce operating costs and expand our ability to deliver educational, scientific, and access-oriented programs.

Current technology resources may include software credits, platform access, cloud tools, design tools, AI-assisted productivity tools, communications tools, and other nonprofit infrastructure support used for administration, education, research coordination, communications, and program development.

Recognition on this page is provided for transparency and appreciation only. It does not imply current sponsorship, endorsement, partnership, control over Foundation activities, approval of Foundation findings, or any right to influence educational content, diagnostic evaluation, publication standards, governance, or program decisions.

Current resources

Technology and infrastructure resources

The Foundation may use a range of nonprofit-access technology resources to support administration, communications, education, research coordination, and program development.

Software, cloud, and productivity tools

Nonprofit software access, cloud credits, productivity tools, design tools, AI-assisted workflow tools, and communications resources help reduce administrative cost and improve program execution.

Program infrastructure support

In-kind equipment, storage, logistics, supplies, and related infrastructure may support educational programming, diagnostic-literacy activities, and access-oriented deployment.

Recognition after verification

Organization names or logos should be listed only when the support is active or historically verified, and logos should be used only when the provider's brand-use rules permit public acknowledgment.

Historical supporters and prior contributors

The Foundation also recognizes organizations and individuals that supported earlier educational, scientific, or charitable activities.

Historical recognition reflects prior support and does not imply current funding, sponsorship, endorsement, partnership, or active affiliation.

Prior educational program experience and grant support are described on the Foundation's mission page for historical context.

Use of names and logos

Use of any company name or logo is subject to applicable grant terms, donor terms, brand guidelines, and Foundation approval. The Foundation does not permit supporter recognition to be used in a way that suggests commercial endorsement, preferred-vendor status, control over Foundation programs, or influence over independent evaluation or publication standards.

Supporter recognition is appreciation-only and does not create governance rights, advisory authority, product endorsement, exclusivity, publication control, or a commercial relationship unless separately documented in writing.

Interested in supporting Foundation infrastructure?

Companies and donors may support the Foundation through charitable contributions, in-kind technology access, equipment donations, consumable donations, or grant-in-aid support aligned with the Foundation's educational and access-oriented mission.
