Diagnostic Validation Program

Independent. Non-commercial. Practitioner-centered.

What It Is

The Aesculapius Foundation's Diagnostic Validation Program provides independent, non-commercial performance evaluation of veterinary point-of-care diagnostic instruments. Studies are conducted in real-world veterinary practice settings using standardized protocols. Findings are published and made available to the veterinary community without charge.

This program fills a gap that manufacturers, distributors, and trade publications cannot fill: truly independent, practice-level performance data on the instruments veterinarians and technicians actually use.

What We Evaluate

  • Analytical accuracy compared to established reference methods
  • Precision and reproducibility across operators, shifts, and time
  • Workflow integration and staff learning curve
  • Veterinary technician usability and ease of daily operation
  • Reagent and consumable handling in a practice environment
  • Result turnaround time and clinical utility in a live practice setting
  • Instrument reliability and maintenance burden

Who Benefits

Veterinary Practices

Access independent performance data before making equipment decisions.

Veterinary Educators

Use validated, non-commercial data in curriculum and professional development materials.

Manufacturers

Independent U.S. practice-level validation data can become a credible third-party performance record.

Distributors and Partners

Validated performance data strengthens the commercial case and supports customer confidence.

Shelters and HVLC Clinics

Access instruments and validated data designed for high-throughput, resource-constrained settings.

A Note on Independence

Manufacturer or distributor participation in a validation study does not influence study design, methodology, data collection, analysis, or findings. The Foundation maintains full editorial and methodological independence. Participants who sponsor or facilitate a study receive no preferential treatment in findings, no right of review prior to publication, and no ability to restrict dissemination of results.

This independence is the foundation of the program's value — to practitioners, to the profession, and to the manufacturers whose instruments perform well under honest scrutiny.
