Program Areas

Building on prior healthcare education experience to support accessible, evidence-informed initiatives in human and veterinary health.

Program Areas

The Aesculapius Foundation has a history of developing and supporting independent healthcare education programs. After a period of dormancy, the Foundation has reactivated its mission with an expanded focus that includes veterinary diagnostic education, independent validation and product evaluation, and access programs for underserved veterinary settings — reflecting the One Health framework that unifies our work.

Veterinary Diagnostic Education

Resources that help veterinary professionals understand diagnostic instrument capabilities, performance characteristics, workflow implications, and clinical utility outside a commercial sales context.

Independent Validation & Product Evaluation

Independent, non-commercial evaluations of veterinary point-of-care diagnostic instruments, related technologies, consumables, workflows, and access-enabling tools in real-world practice settings.

Professional Development

Continuing education, hands-on workshops, webinars, technician training modules, decision-support guides, and professional learning resources.

Access & Equity Initiative

Instrument placement and diagnostic education programs for animal shelters, high-volume low-cost clinics, rural practices, and mobile veterinary services.

Healthcare Education

CME-adjacent programming, health literacy resources, community health education, and professional development consistent with the Foundation's history in human health education.

How We Work

All Foundation programs maintain independence from commercial interests. We do not promote specific products, vendors, or brands. Manufacturer or distributor participation in validation or product evaluation studies does not influence findings or educational content.

Content is grounded in credible evidence, responsible communication, and transparent methodology.

Program principles

  • Independent and educational
  • Evidence-informed
  • Accessible, practical, and useful
  • Collaborative with mission-aligned partners
